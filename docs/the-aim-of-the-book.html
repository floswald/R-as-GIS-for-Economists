R as GIS for Economists

1.1 The aim of the book

This book is being developed as part of my effort to put together course materials for my data science course at University of Nebraska Lincoln for upper-level undergraduate and graduate students. This books aims particularly at spatial data processing for an econometric project, where spatial variables become part of the econometric analysis.1 Over the years, I have seen so many students and researchers who spend so much time just processing spatial data (often involving clicking the ArcGIS (or QGIS) user interface to death), which is a waste of time from the perspective of academic productivity. My hope is that this book helps researchers become more proficient in spatial data processing and enhance the overall productivity of the fields of economics for which spatial data are essential.

  1. This means that this book does not touch on spatial statistics or spatial econometrics at all, which is something you do after you have processed spatial data.↩
